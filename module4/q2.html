Scenario:

In order to promote her bistro, Sookie would like to run a Jelly Bean Guessing Game for her customers. She informs the customer that the number of jelly beans could be anywhere in the range of 2000 to 5000.

Each customer gets 3 attempts to guess the number of jelly beans in the jar, and their result depends on when they guess correctly, if at all. The customer also receives a hint after each guess. The rules of Sookie's game are:

  • If the customer gets it right on their first attempt, they receive a 50% off coupon that they can redeem at their next order.
  • If they fail the first attempt, the customer is given the sum of all the digits of the number of jelly beans as a hint.
  • If the customer is able to guess correctly on the second attempt they get a 25% off coupon for their next order.
  • If they fail the second attempt, the customer is given the first and last digit of the number of jelly beans as the final hint.
  • If the customer is able to guess correctly on the third attempt they get a 10% off coupon for their next order.

Task:

Create a program that helps Sookie run her guessing game. Your program should:

  • Input a customer's guess
  • If the guess is correct, print out the discount the customer will receive on their next order
  • If the guess is incorrect, and not their third guess, print out the hint they should receive and continue receiving their next guess
  • If the guess is incorrect, and is their third guess, print out the discount the customer will receive on their next order
